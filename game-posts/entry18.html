Game Development

Entry 18 

August 5, 2024

This week we were assigned to continue discussing narrative design in one of our favourite games. However, since I already covered this topic last week, I decided to get a head start on next week’s tasks. The upcoming tasks involve two components: a practical one using Unreal Engine 5 (UE5) and a theoretical one, which we’ll continue through slides and discussions on design.

To prepare, I installed UE5 and got more familiar with the interface. Although I have used UE5 in a previous game development course, we only scratched the surface and didn’t write any code. This time, I aimed to dive deeper and explore more of its features.

First, I revisited the basics of the UE5 interface. I explored the different panels, such as the Content Browser, Details panel, and the World Outliner. Understanding these components is crucial for efficient navigation and project management within UE5.

One of the most exciting aspects of UE5 is its Blueprint system, which allows for visual scripting. I created simple Blueprints to get a feel for how they work. This included setting up basic interactions and understanding how to use nodes to control game logic without writing traditional code.

To put my knowledge into practice, I created a simple scene. I added various assets, adjusted their properties, and experimented with lighting and materials. This hands-on approach helped me understand how different elements create a cohesive environment. However, I did not have anything to show for it.

What are your next steps?

Next week, I plan to delve deeper into UE5 by working on more complex Blueprints and starting to write some code. Additionally, I will continue to engage with the theoretical component of our course, focusing on design principles and how they can be applied in practical scenarios.

If you could do this week over, what would you do differently?

If I could do this week over, I would spend more time experimenting with different Blueprint nodes and interactions to understand visual scripting in UE5 better.

Did you give your best effort on this?

I gave my best effort by thoroughly exploring the UE5 interface and experimenting with Blueprints. I aimed to understand each component and how it contributes to the game development process.
