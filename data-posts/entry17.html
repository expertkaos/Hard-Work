During the first week of the new semester, we started with an overview of what we would cover in this class. The assigned work for this week involved delving deeper into Matplotlib and exploring how it organises plots using figures and axes, which is essential for creating detailed visualisations.

I started by exploring how to use different colours for various data series. By applying colours like red, green, and blue to other functions, I could easily distinguish between them, making my plots clearer and more informative.

I also delved into markers and line styles to add more detail to my plots. Using markers such as circles, crosses, squares, and line styles like solid, dashed, and dash-dot helped make my plots more engaging and visually appealing.

One of the highlights was combining these styles to create a more complex plot. I made a plot with a dotted red line and large yellow diamond markers with a green edge. This fun exercise showed how different styles can work together to create an attractive and informative visualisation.

Additionally, I discovered how to set global styles using Matplotlib’s configuration options. This allows me to change the default look of all my plots at once, ensuring a consistent style across my work.

I also explored colourmaps, which are used to represent data with colours. Learning to choose and apply colourmaps will make plots more readable and informative.

Why do you believe we’re studying this?

We’re studying this to enhance our ability to present data clearly and informally. Visualisations are crucial in many fields, and mastering tools like Matplotlib helps us communicate complex data effectively.
