This week was the second week of my project, and I have continued to search for and discover relevant datasets. I came across the Australian Bureau of Statistics(ABS) Census data, which is the data collected from every Australian household every five years. I have explored this data and investigated interesting components to look at.

the link to the data pack which I explored for this week Census DataPacks

In theory, this data should be sufficient for my task, as it has been gathered from all over Australia and includes a data pack worth 3.12 GB. However, there is one problem: the data is divided into sections, with the smallest statistical area being Level One, which has only 61,845 in total across the country. The image provided visualises the SA1 groups around Gungahlin. I believe there is one main reason for using these groups instead of actual data gathered by the Australian Bureau of Statistics. A huge privacy risk is associated with providing access to information about individual households. These sections are suitable for providing somewhat accurate data while protecting privacy rights.
